Introduction
The Streaming Wars
With 1.8 billion+ paid streaming subscriptions worldwide and growing, the OTT market is one of the most competitive consumer landscapes. But what do consumers actually value? Price? Content? Ad-free viewing?
The Question
Which attributes drive streaming platform choice, and how much are consumers willing to pay for each feature?
The Method
Choice-Based Conjoint (CBC) analysis, the gold standard for measuring consumer trade-offs and preferences.
The Goal
Actionable insights for OTT platform managers on pricing, features, and tier design to maximize market share.

What Drives Choice?
Relative Attribute Importance (HB-MNL), computed at the individual level and then averaged.
Price & Ads dominate, together accounting for over 53% of choice variation. Consumers primarily trade off cost vs. ad-free experience.
High heterogeneity: large SDs indicate that not all consumers value the same things. HB captures this individual variation.

Beyond aggregate results, we ran subgroup analyses using individual-level HB betas to test whether preferences differ across segments.

Median split on weekly OTT hours.

Heavy users place more weight on streams and ads, and are slightly less price-sensitive, consistent with multi-device household viewing.

Discussion
Key Takeaways
1

Tiered Pricing Is Essential

Price is the #1 driver (27% RAI). Offer a clear tier ladder: budget, mid-tier, and premium options to capture different willingness-to-pay segments.

2

Invest in Ad-Free

Ads importance matches price (27% RAI). Consumers pay a significant premium for ad-free viewing, with a WTP of +€3.49/month for ad-free vs. ads.

3

Content Breadth Over Exclusivity

"Originals & Licensed" content strongly preferred over both "Licensed Only" and "Exclusive Originals." Breadth wins over exclusivity.

4

The Family Plan Wins

Ad-free + multi-screen + mixed content at €12.99 captures 41% share, driven by shared household viewing.

5

Quality Is a Hygiene Factor

Video quality ranks lowest (15% RAI). Full HD is sufficient for most, and 4K adds only marginal value. Resolution should not come at the expense of content.

6

Segment Your Strategy

High heterogeneity (large SDs in RAI) means one-size-fits-all fails. Some consumers are purely price-driven; others prioritize ad-free or content.
